PRE-SERVICE PREPARATION

Use proper anti-static precautions when performing this replacement. Discharge static electricity before beginning. Work on a static-free surface

Tools Required:

  1. Scissors
  2. 2mm Hex bit or Hex wrench (Allen key)
  3. 2.5mm Hex bit or Hex wrench (Allen key)
  4. 3/32 inches Flathead bit or screwdriver
  5. T10 Torx bit or screwdriver (no longer than 4 inches)
Step 1: Bring Printer to Safe State for Service

  1. Turn on the printer, and verify that the temperature icon reads cool (empty), if it is not cool (full thermometer) allow adequate time for the nozzle and bed to cool.
  2. Turn off the printer.
  3. Unplug the printer.
Step 2: Remove Top Cover on the Extruder

  1. Cut the filament just before the intake on the top of the extruder.
  2. Remove screw located on the right side hole using the T10 Torx screwdriver.
  3. Unscrew the two screws on the top of the filament guide bracket using the 2.5mm Hex wrench.
  4. Remove the top cover.
  5. Carefully unplug filament run out switch from the extruder circuit board, ensuring to pull from the plastic plug and not the wires; pulling the wires can damage the connection to the extruder.
Step 3: Removing the Extruder Motor

  1. Disconnect the white extruder terminal block from the extruder connector as shown in the picture below. Ensure to grab the block and avoid pinching the wires.
  2. Unscrew the two motor screws below using the 2.5 mm Allen key. Ensure that the screws once fully unscrewed, should still be left inserted in the extruder chassis hole.
  3. Pull the extruder motor assembly away from the printer (vertically).
Step 4: Nozzle Assembly Removal

  1. Unscrew the nozzle assembly screw with 2mm Hex wrench as shown in the picture below, there is an access pass-through for this screw located just to the right of the blue level sensor motor. This is a set screw, loosen and do not remove completely. The leveling arm may need to be moved to access the set screw.
  2. Unscrew the Torx screws that hold the fan baffle assembly and pull down the assembly.
  3. Unplug the thermocouple connector (white) from the extruder circuit board.
  4. Use flathead screw driver to disconnect heater wires from extruder circuit board. Gently pull the wires out of the green terminal block.
  5. Push down nozzle tube and pull from bottom. (Note: Ensure that sufficient time has been given for the nozzle to cool).
Step 5: Insert New Nozzle Assembly

  1. Route the heater and the thermocouple wires through the passage in the chassis.
  2. Bend the white heater wires, insert in the terminal block and tighten the flathead screws. Gently pull the wires to check if the wires are securely held in place.
  3. Plug in the white thermocouple connector in the extruder circuit board.
  4. Push the nozzle assembly in the extruder chassis hole such that the heater block is in the left hand side.
  5. Tighten the nozzle assembly screw with 2mm Hex wrench.
  6. Lineup the air baffle assembly with extruder chassis, with the hole towards the user (front).Insert the fan baffle assembly and match the screw holes with the holes on the chassis. Screw the 2 Torx screws to tighten the fan baffle assembly.
Step 6: Replace Extruder Motor

  1. Place the extruder motor on the chassis. Extruder motor screw holes should line up with holes in the chassis.
  2. Tighten the two hex screws with 2.5mm Hex wrench.
  3. Plug in the white terminal block on the extruder connector.
Step 7: Replace Top Cover

  1. Attach the filament runout switch wires of the cover to the extruder circuit board.
  2. Place the new top cover over the extruder.
  3. Replace the two 2mm screws onto the cover.
Step 8: Test the Machine

  1. Plug in and turn on the 3D45.
  2. Navigate to “Filament” and follow the on screen instructions to load filament.
  3. Build the “Test Print” file on the machine to ensure the 3D45 printer is working correctly.

  3. Plug fan connector into circuit board.
